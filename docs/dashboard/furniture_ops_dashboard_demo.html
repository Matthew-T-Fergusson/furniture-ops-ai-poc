Operational commentary: anomalies, trends, outstanding work

COO read: the operation is working — items are selling, gross margins are attractive, and May shows better expense control than the first ramp month. The weak spot is not demand proof; it is throughput discipline after acquisition.

What is going well: revenue has been consistent across the first three operating months, gross margin percentage stayed strong, and April/May cash flow turned positive after March’s startup inventory build. That suggests the basic buy/list/sell model is viable.

What is going badly or needs pressure: aging inventory is becoming the main operating risk. 71 active listings are 30+ days old and 32 are 60+ days old, representing roughly $86.7k and $43.9k of asking value respectively. That is too much value sitting in listings without a refresh/markdown/relist decision.

Recent accomplishments: the dashboard itself, analytics views, inventory pipeline, and DB-backed operating metrics are now usable enough to manage from. That is a real step up from spreadsheet-only tracking.

Bottleneck: Jira shows too many important AWF items still in backlog, especially compliance/banking/bookkeeping and listing-prep tasks. The operating cadence should move from “build the system” to “use the system weekly to force decisions.”

Stale status items — what this means

No stale workflow-status rows are currently showing, which is good. But this may be a false sense of cleanliness because the bigger stale signal is in active listings by days listed. The status workflow is not the bottleneck right now; listing aging is.

A stale-status item is inventory that has sat too long in its current workflow state based on operational thresholds: pending sale ≥7 days, ready to list ≥14 days, listed active ≥30 days, hold ≥14 days, acquired/refurb needed ≥21 days. This is an action queue: follow up, markdown/relist, move to next status, or confirm it is intentionally parked.
